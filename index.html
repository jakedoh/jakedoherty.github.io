Jake Doherty

Numerical Relativity • Black Holes • Exotic Compact Objects

I am a researcher at the University of Texas at Austin working in numerical relativity and computational gravitational physics, with interests in black hole spacetimes, scalar fields, exotic compact objects, hyperbolic PDEs, and relativistic simulations.

My current work focuses on solving Einstein's equations in 3+1 form, evolving compact objects numerically, and studying nonlinear dynamics in strong gravity.

Research

Black Hole Initial Data

Constructing constraint-satisfying initial data with linear and angular momentum, including generalized conformal decompositions and analytic tensor structures.

Scalar Field Dynamics

Numerical evolution of self-gravitating scalar fields and boson stars, and black holes immersed in scalar field environments.

Relativistic Wave Equations

Solving Regge–Wheeler and related perturbation equations for compact-object spacetimes.

Projects

3+1 Einstein Evolution Code

Developing a numerical relativity infrastructure for evolving black hole and scalar field spacetimes.

Spectral Boson Star Solver

Newton iteration and spectral collocation methods for binary boson star solutions.

Gravitational Wave Ringdown Analysis

Fourier and overtone analysis of damped oscillations in perturbed black hole geometries.
